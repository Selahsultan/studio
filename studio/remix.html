My original inquiry examined how the idea of metamorphosis shapes our understanding of self and identity. With this Remix, I aimed to push that inquiry further, exploring transformation as an ongoing, deeply internalized dialogue rather than merely a visible change. I chose collage as my medium because its fragmented nature authentically captures the complexity of evolving identitiesâpieces of surrealism, anatomy, poetry, and popular culture merge, much like the diverse aspects that form a person's sense of self.

Compositionally, each choice carried intention: anatomical imagery symbolizes the painful, raw aspects of growth; butterflies underscore beauty born from struggle; cameras represent self-perception and external judgment; surrealist motifs remind us of the ambiguity and uncertainty intrinsic to personal transformation. This remix offers a perspective that transformation is neither linear nor simpleâit's an intricate process embedded with discomfort and beauty, confusion and clarity. Ultimately, it invites viewers to embrace change as essential to human existence.
